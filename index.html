«The concept that women and men have equal conditions, treatment and opportunities for realizing their full potential, human rights and dignity, and for contributing to (and benefitting from) economic, social, cultural and political development. Gender equality is, therefore, the equal valuing by society of the similarities and the differences of men and women, and the roles they play. It is based on women and men being full partners in the home, community and society. Equality does not mean that women and men will become the same but that women’s and men’s rights, responsibilities and opportunities will not depend on whether they are born male or female. Gender equality implies that the interests, needs and priorities of both women and men and girls Gender equality is a matter of human rights and is considered a precondition for, and indicator of, sustainable people-centred development. »
Glossary of Terms and Concepts — UNICEF.

Knowledge discovery

Although this concept may seem self-evident, gender equality is still a goal to be achieved. What needs to be changed is an entrenched social system, which is why gender equality needs constant effort to stop being an issue. Since 2013 the European Institute for Gender Equality (EIGE) draws up an annual report regarding progress and setbacks of women’s role in society. Six domains are considered: work, money, knowledge, time, power, health. The score goes from 1 to 100, that would mean the full equality between women and man in all these aspects. In 2022, Italy ranks 14th in the general index with a score of 65.0. The best score regards Health: 89.0, the worst one concerns Power, with a score of 56.9. This latter calculates the political, social, and economic spheres. Although all domains taken in consideration by the EIGE are crucial, we decided to analyze the worst one: Power. Moreover, as we stated before, to achieve a change of course in gender equity the overall mindset must be converted. From our perspective, the political realm should be the starting point for an unbiased nation. Furthermore, it is what mirrors the social structure and level of progress of the country itself. hence, this is what the Chamber of Deputies’ project is based on.

Examining Women's Representation in Italian Politics: Hypothesis-Driven Analysis

This study employs a hypothesis-driven analysis to investigate the representation of women in the Italian political scene, with a specific focus on the Chamber of Deputies. By formulating a hypothesis based on existing knowledge and theories concerning gender disparities in politics, the study suggests that women are likely to be underrepresented among deputies and may hold less influential roles. Through the systematic collection and analysis of data, the aim is to either support or challenge this hypothesis. By rigorously examining patterns, trends, and factors that contribute to the underrepresentation of women in positions of power within the political sphere, valuable insights will be gained into the current state of gender equality within the Chamber of Deputies. This analysis also contributes to the broader conversation on achieving gender parity in political leadership.

The Chamber of Deputies: A Reflection of Gender Dynamics in Italian Society

The focus of this analysis lies primarily on the Chamber of Deputies, the most representative body in Italy's political system. As the lower house of the Parliament, it wields significant influence in shaping legislation, policy decisions, and governance. By specifically examining the representation and roles of women in the Chamber of Deputies, this study seeks to address the gender disparities prevalent within this influential institution. It is important to recognize that the political realm often mirrors the broader societal dynamics, and any imbalances or inequalities witnessed within the political sphere reflect underlying issues within Italian society. Thus, an analysis of women's representation in the Chamber of Deputies provides insights into the wider gender dynamics and power structures at play. Addressing these disparities within the political domain becomes crucial in fostering greater gender equality and driving social progress. The examination of the Chamber of Deputies contributes to the overarching efforts aimed at creating a more inclusive and equitable society for all.
