Geographical Distribution of Male and Female Deputies
Uncovering Regional Representation Disparities
What is the geographical distribution of male and female deputies? Analysis through the italian regions.

The geographical distribution of male and female deputies in the Italian Chamber of Deputies is a key aspect to consider in assessing regional representation within the institution.
This question aims to explore the regional origins of deputies and investigate potential correlations between geographical distribution and gender representation.
The hypothesis posits that there may be a higher concentration of deputies coming from the northern regions of Italy, due to the sizable socio-economic development.
The choropleth map presents a visual representation of the data, allowing us to compare and contrast the number of male and female deputies in each region. Puglia: Puglia has a high number of male deputies, with 369 representatives. However, the number of female deputies

is considerably lower at 12, representing approximately 3.15% of the total. Lazio: Lazio has a comparatively high number of male deputies, with 438 representatives. In terms of female representation, Lazio fares better with 91 deputies, accounting for approximately 17.18% of the total, the highest percentage for female provenance. Examining the remaining regions, we observe varying levels of gender representation.

Examining the remaining regions, we observe varying levels of gender representation. Some regions, such as Trentino-Alto Adige/Südtirol and Basilicata, have relatively low overall representation, both for male and female deputies.
Other regions, like Emilia-Romagna and Veneto, have a more balanced representation, with approximately 10.60% and 9.12% of deputies being female, respectively.

Smaller representation is recorded in Valle d'Aosta (14 male, 1 female) and in Molise (40 male, 2 female); this specific result is surely also due to the demography of the two regions, that are the smallest e less crowded in Italy. Upon examining the data, several observations can be made: Lombardia: Lombardia has the highest number of male deputies, with 657 representatives. In fact, Lombardy is the biggest and most populated region in Italy. Despite this,

the number of women is much lower than men, namely 62.Hence, the female presence is approximately 8.6% and 91.4% male presence. This makes Lombardy third in terms of female provenance after Lazio and Tuscany, whose population is approximatively 1/2 and 1/3 of Lombardy population. This latter discrepancy may be attributed to various factors, however it devalues the starting hypotesis.
Other leading regions for male presence

are Campania counting 544 and Sicilia counting 495. Correspondingly, 47 and 35 female deputies.

North, center, south

In conclusion, when the regions are not considered singularly, but clusterd by northern, central, and southern regions of Italy, we can observe variations in the percentages of female deputies.

In the North,(Piemonte, Lombardia, Veneto, Emilia-Romagna, Liguria, Trentino-Alto Adige/Südtirol, Friuli-Venezia Giulia, Valle d'Aosta) approximately 34.41% of the deputies are female, indicating a relatively higher representation compared to the Central and South regions. The Central regions (Toscana, Marche, Umbria, Lazio) has approximately 27.02% female deputies, while the South regions (Campania, Abruzzo, Puglia, Sicilia, Calabria, Basilicata, Sardegna, Molise) has the lowest representation at approximately 8.32%.
