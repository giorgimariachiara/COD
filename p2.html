Geographical Distribution of Male and Female Deputies
Uncovering Regional Representation Disparities

The geographical distribution of male and female deputies in the Italian Chamber of Deputies is a key aspect to consider in assessing regional representation within the institution. This question aims to explore the regional origins of deputies and investigate potential correlations between geographical distribution and gender representation. The hypothesis posits that there may be a higher concentration of deputies coming from the northern regions of Italy. Analyzing the provided data on male and female representation across different regions, we can gain valuable insights into the geographical distribution of deputies. The choropleth map presents a visual representation of the data, allowing us to compare and contrast the number of male and female deputies in each region.

Examining the remaining regions, we observe varying levels of gender representation.
Some regions, such as Trentino-Alto Adige/Südtirol and Basilicata, have relatively low overall representation, both for male and female deputies.
Other regions, like Emilia-Romagna and Veneto, have a more balanced representation, with approximately 10.60% and 9.12% of deputies being female, respectively.
North, center, south

When considering the North, Central, and South regions of Italy, we can observe variations in the percentages of female deputies. In the North, approximately 34.41% of the deputies are female, indicating a relatively higher representation compared to the Central and South regions. The Central region has approximately 27.02% female deputies, while the South region has the lowest representation at approximately 8.32%. Overall, the data suggests that while there may be variations in gender representation among different regions, the hypothesis of a higher concentration of deputies from the northern regions is not entirely confirmed. While some regions show a more balanced gender representation, others exhibit clear gender disparities.

Upon examining the data, several observations can be made:
Lombardia: Lombardia has the highest number of male deputies, with 657 representatives. Meanwhile, it also has a relatively significant number of female deputies, totaling 62. This suggests a relatively balanced representation in this region, with approximately 9.45% of deputies being female.
Puglia: Puglia has a high number of male deputies, with 369 representatives. However, the number of female deputies is considerably lower at 12, representing approximately 3.15% of the total.
Lazio: Lazio has a relatively high number of male deputies, with 438 representatives. In terms of female representation, Lazio fares better with 91 deputies, accounting for approximately 17.18% of the total.
