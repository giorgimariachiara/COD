Gender Disparities in the Italian Chamber of Deputies
Analyzing Representation Patterns and Progress
How many deputies are there in the Chamber of Deputies? Analysis based on gender and legislature.

The horizontal bar chart provides a detailed breakdown of the representation of male and female deputies in the Italian Chamber of Deputies throughout various

legislative terms. Examining the data reveals interesting patterns and disparities in gender representation within the institution.
In the earlier legislatures, such as the Costituente and Legislature I, the number of female deputies was relatively low, with 21 and 45 representatives respectively. Over time, the numbers fluctuated, but there was a general upward trend in female representation.

Notably, the Legislature XVIII saw the highest number of female deputies with 240 representatives, while the XIX Legislature had 129. The widest gender gap was observed in Legislature XVII, where there where 206 male members and only 466 female members. To gain a better understanding of the gender disparities, it is helpful to also calculate the percentages of some significant data. For instance, the Legislature XVII,

records the highest gender inbalance, with 69.35% of male presence, and 30,65% of female. The highest percentage of female representation in the chart is found in the XVIII Legislature, where out of a total of 660 deputies, women represented approximately 36.36% of the representatives. On the other hand, the lowest percentage is observed in the Costituente, with women comprising

around 3.62% of the 581 deputies. While progress has been made in some legislatures, there are instances where female representation remains significantly low. In summary, the data presented in the chart demonstrates a significant increase in female representation from Legislature I to Legislature XVIII. owever, there was a sharp decline in female representation in Legislature XIX, which warrants further investigation into potential reasons for this drop.
